Urgent / Airbnb Cases
Complaint Workflow
When to use this: Use this process when a case is very urgent — most commonly Airbnb bookings. Because Airbnb will refund the guest regardless, we risk getting delisted and penalised for unresolved guest issues.

This process involves refunding the guest before getting partner approval. We do this because partners often respond slowly, which leads to upset guests, negative reviews, and unnecessary stress on the team. Acting fast protects both the guest experience and our platform standing.
🌠
Stage 1 — Guest Complaint Review (Agents)
An automatic task will be assigned to you with reminders on what to action. All tasks must be completed and fault assigned. Sort out the guest issue first, then assess compensation. Contact partner if partner fault is identified.
🤸
Stage 2 — Escalated (TL / Manager — Jannine & Jada)
Only move to TL stage when the guest is still unhappy after the maximum compensation within the profit margin has been offered. TL reviews the ticket, validates fault and compensation, and decides whether to close or escalate further to AM.
🛠️
Stage 3 — Pending Recovery (Account Managers)
AM reviews the case and makes the recovery decision. Only move to this stage if the loss to Jetstream is larger than $100 due to a partner fault error.
⚠️ Note: Please move to this stage only if the loss to Jetstream is larger than $100 due to a partner fault error.
⚠️ Note: Cases remain in this stage for a minimum of four weeks before aging and moving to Closed.
📦
Stage 4 — Closed: Jetstream Loss
Tickets can be moved here for any Jetstream loss that is less than $100 or when a case above $100 has completed the Pending Recovery stage and the AM task is closed with recovery refused, waived, or written off.
An AM task is still created on partner-fault cases under $100 for visibility.
📦
Stage 5 — Closed: Jetstream Win
Move here when the partner recovered compensation completely, OR no compensation was required to resolve the ticket. Jetstream cost = $0.
